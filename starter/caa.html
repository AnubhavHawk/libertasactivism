Citzenship Ammendment Act

  • Posted on 30th May, 2021

  • By Lavanya Vinayak

  • Published in History

  • 3 min read

the Citizenship Amendment Act or the CAA is an act passed by the Indian Parliament on December 11th, 2019. This act amended the 1995 CAA.In brief, this bill provides a path to citizenship of non-Muslim migrants from Pakistan, Afghanistan, and Bangladesh, who came to India before December 31st, 2014. It provides citizenship to all illegal immigrants from all religions except Muslims.
WHAT ARE SOME RIGHTS THAT WE HAVE BEING CITIZENS OF INDIA? Apart from voting rights, we as citizens have fundamental rights like freedom of speech, equality, choice, freedom of assembly, and such. Also the right to reside in India. Whereas the foreigners are entitled only to personal liberty and fundamental rights. The Citizenship Amendment Act (CAA), amends who can be an Indian citizen- any foreigner applying for Indian citizenship that is. All world religions talk about world peace and harmony with everyone. They propagate universal love and brotherhood. In the preamble of the Indian Constitution, the Republic of India declares itself as a sovereign, socialist, secular and democratic republic. Note what it says- India is a secular nation. Secularism in actuality, means that no one is allowed to publicly practice or display religious icons and symbols to ensure harmony on religious grounds, as there have been no provocative theological actions. Naturally, we Indians have added our own desi meaning to the phrase when we got independence from the British. Indian Secularism means treating all religions equally, in society, in front of the administration and the courts of law. This is where CAA’s problems start. It is simply not a secular act. In the many clauses, it states that people of all faiths- Hindu, Buddhist, Jain, Sikh, Jew and Christian can apply for Indian Citizenship. Feel something is missing, well Muslims can’t apply for citizenship of this law.We must realise, in today’s world, terrorism is a large and growing threat. Many of the victims of this fear are innocent Muslims who are killed and Islamophobia is created in the world. To sensitise the world to their problems, is it not imperative we give the people in such conflict-prone areas a good life. Which can be achieved if they can access Indian citizenship. Frankly, as someone said- there is unity in diversity and as Indians we all know that we have a very accepting culture of assimilation, absorbing multiple foreign communities into our own so you can’t tell the difference.
THE PROBLEM: It goes against the very Constitution of the country by violating secularism.The ruling party offered asylum to persecuted minorities. But the CAA specifies who is being persecuted.It requires the migrants to produce documents. Not ID cards or tax slips but ancestral documents which could have been easily destroyed or lost.If we put CAA and NRC together, Hindus, Christians, Sikhs, Jains, etc. without documents, are illegal immigrants under NRC but will be granted citizenship under CAA.But Muslims will lose their voting rights, land, and other property
JUSTIFICATION GIVEN BY THE RULING PARTY: Violation of Article14 (equality before law)- They justified it by calling it “reasonable classification” according to which “if there is a special law then the court gets to decide whether the classification is reasonable (emphasis required)”Violation of Article 25 (freedom of religion)- It argues that it aims to protect the religious rights of “persecuted minorities” seeking Indian citizenship. They stated that it does not affect the worship and religious beliefs of Muslims.In its defence, the Central govt has stated that Pakistan, Afghanistan and Bangladesh are Muslim-majority countries therefore Muslims are "unlikely to face religious persecution" there.The CAA goes against the very core principles of the Constitution: secularism which is mentioned in the Preamble itself. The CAA also happens to be the first religion-based law that has been passed in the history of India. CAA violates Constitutional secular principles and is a violation of Articles 13, 14, 15, 16 and 21 which guarantee the right to equality, equality before the law and non-discriminatory treatment by the Indian State. This also comes at a time when Islamophobia is rampant in India, making it more difficult for Muslims to cement their status as legitimate citizens of our country.While the Central Government has tried to justify its actions by stating that Pakistan, Afghanistan and Bangladesh are Muslim-majority countries therefore Muslims are "unlikely to face religious persecution" there, they have failed to consider that sects like Shias and Ahmedis also face persecution in Muslim-majority countries like Pakistan but are not included in the CAA.Questions were also raised on the exclusion of persecuted religious minorities from other regions such as Tibet, Sri Lanka and Myanmar such as the Rohingyas (a persecuted Muslim minority in Myanmar). In retaliation, Union Home Minister Amit Shah had said in Parliament that “Rohingya will never be accepted as citizens of India” which shows the deteriorating treatment of Muslim refugees in India.
