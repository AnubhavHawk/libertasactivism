The Trial Of Chicago 7

  • Posted on 30th May, 2021

  • By Arsh Arora

  • Published in History

  • 1 min read

The Trial of Chicago 8 relates to the trial of Mr. Tom Hayden, Mr. Abbie Hoffman, Mr. Dave Dellinger, Mr. Jerry Rubin, Mr. Rennie Davis, Mr. Bobby Seale, Mr. Lee Weiner and Dr. John Froines represented by Adv. Kunstler in the court of Judge Julius Hoffman regarding their involvement in riots in Chicago as a response to the country’s involvement in the Vietnam War. The gatherings held by the accused were supposed to be peaceful but instead they ended up being riots. The seven accused were arrested because of their speeches and cries on the day of the gatherings which supposedly incited the crowd. The case presented by the DoJ was severely twisted against the defendants to support the political alignment of the Attorney General of the Country.

The Trial started with 8 people who all were treated in a hostile manner and due to the severe racism in the 60’s in USA, Mr. Seale, the only black defendant, was kept under police arrest and was severely mistreated even during the trial. Mr. Seale did not take representation under Kunstler and due to lack of lawyer due to health conditions Mr. Seale did not get the opportunity to speak at his own trial and the Judge, who is now recognised as one of the worst judges of the Federal courts of US History. There was even a brief moment when out of protest, Mr. Seale received 14 counts of contempt and was then bound and gagged in the middle of the court and was in a condition even a dictatorship would not permit. Seeing this the DoJ moved Mr. Seale out of the class and was given a mistrial for his treatment. This made the Chicago 8, the Chicago 7.

During the trial the defence was heavily ridiculed and undermined by the Judge which made the case a matter of national importance but it wasn't virtually possible for anyone to intervene considering the system of courts of the USA. In the trial the DoJ only called upon government officials for being witnesses and no witness presented by the defence was directly presented before of the jury, this also included the Mr. Ramsey Clark, former attorney general of the USA. In this trial there was even an instance when the DoJ presented a false sentence from Tom Hayden in which he was supposedly saying “If Blood is going to flow, let it flow all over the city”. This statement was used out of context, it showed that the people must attack the police, rather he meant to say that if the police is going to attack them, then they should spread out all over the city so that the world can watch this brutality. Such incidents worsened the case for the Prosecution but Judge Hoffman still did not count any contempt or penalties on them making the defence to lose faith in the court and disrespect the institutions. Finally after this the defence discontinued to present facts and requested for the verdict.

In the verdict, Mr. Weiner and Dr. Froines were acquitted while the five were given imprisonment for 5 years. Although they were acquitted soon and they all went on to live prosperous lives as authors, politicians and professors. This incident changed American History in ways unimaginable by many. The incident changed the precedents of American Trial Laws and also changed the nations inclination towards involvement in the Vietnam War leading to President Nixon retrieving from Vietnam very soon. But unfortunately by then approximately 4700 Americans had sacrificed themselves in Vietnam for a war which wasn't ever theirs.

The Prosecution framed me and Lee to make the other 5 look worse .... anyways I have always felt that the 5 months we spent in those unjust walls changed things like no politician ever could have.
Dr. John Froines
